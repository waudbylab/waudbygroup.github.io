Preprint: Kinetic NMR screening using 19F relaxation dispersion

24 Sep 2025

Congratulations to PhD student Sophia Hazlett on her first manuscript, now available as a preprint on ChemRxiv! This work demonstrates how kinetic 19F NMR methods, commonly used for studying protein dynamics, can accelerate the analysis and screening of small molecule interactions in fragment mixtures.

The lab's first home-grown manuscript describes a new conceptual approach to NMR fragment screening. Easy-to-use software implementing these methods is available as part of our NMRAnalysis.jl package. Read the preprint here.

Gold LEAF award for the second year running!

Jun 2025

We're thrilled to announce that the Waudby Group has been awarded a Gold LEAF award for the second consecutive year! LEAF (Laboratory Efficiency Assessment Framework) is a standard developed at UCL for improving laboratory sustainability and efficiency.

Laboratory research is essential but also extremely resource-intensive, responsible for around 2% of global plastic waste and using 3-10 times more energy per square meter than typical offices. We encourage other labs to join the LEAF programme and help create more sustainable research environments. Learn more about our sustainability initiatives.

UCL School of Pharmacy hosts national NMR meetings

16-17 Jun 2025

The UCL School of Pharmacy hosted the Royal Society of Chemistry NMR Discussion Group's Magnetic Resonance Managers' Meeting and Postgraduate Meeting, bringing together approximately 90 attendees from across the UK magnetic resonance community. The meetings featured keynote lectures from Dr Miguel Mompeán and Professor Sasi Conte, alongside eleven student talks and many poster and flash presentations demonstrating the high calibre of early career researchers in the field.

It was incredibly rewarding to see the next generation of NMR spectroscopists here learning and building their community. These meetings demonstrate the continuing importance of magnetic resonance techniques across fields from carbon capture and energetic materials to drug discovery and development.

PhD students present at NMR meeting in Paris

26-28 May 2025

PhD students Kleopatra Savvidou and Sophia Hazlett attended the XIVth 'NMR: a tool for biology' meeting at the Pasteur Institute in Paris. Both students presented posters describing their research developing new NMR methods for fragment screening, and on structural biology of calreticulin, a component of the ER glycan quality control pathway.

It was a great opportunity for our students to share their work with the international NMR community and learn about the latest developments in biological NMR spectroscopy. Find out more about our group members.

AAT polymer study published in Science Advances

7 May 2025

Congratulations to first author Sarah Lowen on the publication of her study "High-resolution characterization of ex vivo AAT polymers by solution-state NMR spectroscopy" in Science Advances! The product of a close collaboration between the Waudby group and the Lomas group (UCL Medicine), this remarkable work demonstrates the power of solution-state NMR to study large disease-associated polymers at natural abundance derived from patient liver explants.

Dynamic and Assembly Characteristics of a Deep‐Cavity Basket

30 Oct 2023

Our latest paper, in collaboration with the Bajdic group, reports the characterisation of a supramolecular host with a deep cavity interacting with the drug mitoxanthrone, using a combination of NMR lineshape analysis and CEST to understand the internal dynamics, and the competition between self-recognition (dimerisation) and guest binding.
